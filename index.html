US Government UAP Data Sources

All government-sourced sightings on this map are drawn from officially released documents and reports.

AARO — All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office

Established 2022. Responsible for detecting, identifying, and attributing UAP. Has catalogued hundreds of reports from military personnel. Released Historical Record Reports (2024).

  • AARO Historical Record Report Vol. 1 & 2 (2024)
  • Ongoing case database (partially declassified)

UAP Task Force / UAPTF (2020–2022)

Pentagon task force that produced the landmark June 2021 "Preliminary Assessment: Unidentified Aerial Phenomena" report covering 144 incidents from 2004–2021.

Congressional UAP Hearings (2022–2024)

House and Senate hearings included testimony from military pilots and intelligence officials. Key testimony from Ryan Graves, David Fravor, and David Grusch (2023).

Declassified Videos

Three videos officially declassified by the DoD (2020):

  • FLIR1 / "Tic Tac" — USS Nimitz, Nov 2004, off San Diego, CA
  • GIMBAL — 2015, off Florida Atlantic coast
  • GOFAST — 2015, off Florida Atlantic coast

Project Blue Book (1952–1969)

US Air Force investigation of 12,618 reports. Now fully declassified. 701 cases remain "unidentified." Available via National Archives.

NUFORC — National UFO Reporting Center

Civilian database with 170,000+ reports since 1974. Frequently cited in government briefings.

FAA Pilot Reports

Pilots file reports through FAA channels. Selected cases have been referenced in AARO and UAPTF reports.
